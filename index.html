My Background in Three Paragraphs

After graduating high school in 2019 I landed an IT-related internship at my local Sheriff's Office. At the time, I had no interest in pursuing a degree in CS as I was majoring in Psychology and hoping to apply that to a career with law enforcement.

After a few months of working at this internship, I started watching training videos CompTIA Sec+ and this was my first real exposure to CS. After this, I switched my major to CS and began pursuing it. I later transferred from Hartnell Community College to CSUMB where I am currently attending. Since watching those first CompTIA Sec+ training videos, I have kept an interest in computer networks and cybersecurity, even though most of my coursework has been focused on programming. I believe this is why I am drawn to the field of reverse engineering.

At CSUMB I was presented with more opportunites to get involved in CS projects and develop skills not taught in the courses I was taking. This leads us to today, where I am conducting undergraduate research and am juggling several different projects related to my interests in CS.

My favorite classes that I have completed so far: Introduction to Computer Networks, Algorithms and Algorithm Design, Internet Programming, Software Engineering, and Data Structures.
